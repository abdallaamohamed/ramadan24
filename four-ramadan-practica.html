Four Ramadan Practica, as Articulated by Imam Sohaib Sultan

Notes by Nabil Shaikh (Ramadan 1439, revised Ramadan 1445)

Ramadan Practicum #1: Building Character

  1. Identify a habit that constitutes a major moral / spiritual obstacle between you and God. This is your biggest character flaw. Consider the inward and outward dimensions of this obstacle.
  2. Write down the Negative Thoughts from which this habit begins, for indeed negative habits begin with negative thoughts. Then, write down the Positive Thoughts with which you will replace these Negative Thoughts when they arise.
  3. Write down the Negative Company that is complicit in producing this habit, then write down the Positive Company with which you will replace it. (Note: "company" does not have to be people, but can include certain places, websites, and environments too.)
  4. Write down the Negative Action that you take, and replace it with a Positive Action.
  5. Write down the Negative Gratification you receive from doing the negative act, and replace with a form of Positive Gratification that you will reward yourself with after the positive act.

Ramadan Practicum #3: Developing a Relationship with the Qur'an

Our right over the Qur'an is that it guides us. The Qur'an's right over us is fivefold:

  1. That we recite it.
  2. That we memorize it.
  3. That we reflect on it.
  4. That we live it.
  5. That we teach it.

This practicum has five steps, in line with these five rights:

  1. Come up with a Ramadan plan for recitation.
  2. Come up with a Ramadan plan for memorization.
  3. Come up with a Ramadan plan for contemplation and reflection.
  4. Come up with a Ramadan plan for living and implementing.
  5. Come up with a Ramadan plan for teaching.

Ramadan Practicum #4: Constructing Your Personalized Dua

Dua is the weapon of the believer. It is our way out of danger and desperation.

Part A: Fostering a Daily State of Dua

  1. Pick one time during each day of Ramadan, and set a timer for 5-10 minutes
  2. Just sit for that timespan, with your hands up, palms facing the heavens.
  3. Verbalize a dua, or don't verbalize it, but just sit there in a state of dua.

Part B: Writing a Personalized Ramadan Dua

Follow the circle below. Write this dua on paper or on your phone. Add to it during the month.

  1. Start with duas for your own Self.
  2. Next, include duas for your family.
  3. Continue with duas for your community, your neighbors, your friends.
  4. Lastly, conclude with duas for the world, for social justice, for the oppressed, and for non-persons too - i.e. the animals, ecosystems, the universe.
